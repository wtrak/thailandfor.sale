Frequently Asked Questions

Can foreigners own property in Koh Samui?

Foreigners cannot directly own land in Thailand but can own buildings and condos. Common solutions include leasehold, company structures, and condo ownership under the 49% quota.

What’s the buying process?

Most transactions involve a deposit, legal due diligence, sales agreement, and final transfer at the Land Office. Trusted lawyers and developers make the process straightforward.

Are properties good investments?

Yes. Villas and condos in desirable areas can generate 6–12% yields when managed professionally, alongside lifestyle use.

What about healthcare, utilities, and living costs?

Koh Samui has modern private hospitals, reliable utilities, and a cost of living lower than most Western countries.
